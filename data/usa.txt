Title: United States Gross Domestic Product (GDP) Overview

The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of the United States is a key indicator of the country's economic performance. As of 2023, the nominal GDP of the U.S. was approximately $26.95 trillion, making it the largest economy in the world.

GDP represents the total market value of all final goods and services produced in a country during a specific period. It is often measured quarterly and annually by the Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA).

Key Highlights:

1947 GDP: $243.1 billion

1980 GDP: $2.86 trillion

2000 GDP: $10.25 trillion

2010 GDP: $14.96 trillion

2020 GDP (pandemic impact): $20.93 trillion

2023 GDP: $26.95 trillion

Real vs. Nominal GDP:

Nominal GDP is not adjusted for inflation and reflects current market prices.

Real GDP is adjusted for inflation and reflects the actual volume of production.

GDP Components:

Consumer Spending (C): The largest component, including goods and services.

Investment (I): Business expenditures on capital and construction.

Government Spending (G): Federal, state, and local expenditures.

Net Exports (X-M): Exports minus imports.

Recent Trends:

The U.S. economy rebounded after COVID-19 with growth driven by technology, services, and consumer spending.
